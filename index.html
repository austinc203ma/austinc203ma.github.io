My Deals: Unified Omnichannel Promotions
Staples.com — Dotcom & Retail Integration
$7.2M
Incremental Revenue
The Problem
Staples operates as two legally separate businesses — its dotcom storefront and its physical retail chain. Customers crossing between them encountered entirely different promotional experiences, creating friction and missed revenue opportunities. There was no single view of "your deals."
My Approach
As PM for promotions across both business units, I led cross-functional alignment with merchandising, digital experience, and VP-level leadership. The core challenge was architectural: building a personalization layer that could intelligently classify each visitor — dotcom customer, retail customer, or omni shopper — and serve the most relevant offers for each segment.
The Outcome
Launched "My Deals" on Staples.com — a personalized promotions hub that surfaces the right offer to the right customer. The experience consolidated retail and dotcom promotions under a unified interface, driving $7.2M in incremental sales across the promotions portfolio.
Operational Efficiency · Systems Design
Retail Promotions Floor Plan Automation
Staples Retail — 980 Stores Nationwide
$2.5M+
Labor Cost Savings (Est.)
The Problem
Five times a year, Staples launches a major seasonal promotional campaign across its nearly 980 retail stores. Each transition required five full-time employees to manually reassign every store's promotional floor plan — a process that consumed roughly one work week per campaign cycle, totaling five weeks of high-effort, low-value labor annually.
My Approach
I identified this as a systems problem masquerading as a staffing problem. Rather than accepting the manual process, I worked with engineering to design a proxy environment where the retail team could plan and map their seasonal floor layouts in advance. On campaign go-live, teams simply switched the active feed — no manual reassignment, no transition period.
The Outcome
Overnight store transitions, fully automated. All 980 stores receive their updated promotional layouts simultaneously at go-live. Five weeks of annual labor across five $100K+ employees was effectively eliminated — freeing the team to focus on strategy rather than execution overhead.
