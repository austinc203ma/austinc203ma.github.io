My Deals: Unified Omnichannel Promotions
Staples.com — Dotcom & Retail Integration
$7.2M
Incremental Revenue
The Problem
Staples operates as two legally separate businesses — its dotcom storefront and its physical retail chain. Customers crossing between them encountered entirely different promotional experiences, creating friction and missed revenue opportunities. There was no single view of "your deals."
My Approach
As PM for promotions across both business units, I led cross-functional alignment with merchandising, digital experience, and VP-level leadership. The core challenge was architectural: building a personalization layer that could classify each visitor — dotcom, retail, or omni shopper — and serve the most relevant offers per segment.
The Outcome
Launched "My Deals" on Staples.com — a personalized promotions hub that surfaces the right offer to the right customer. The experience consolidated retail and dotcom promotions under a unified interface, driving $7.2M in incremental sales across the promotions portfolio.

Operational Efficiency · Systems Design
Retail Promotions Floor Plan Automation
Staples Retail — 980 Stores Nationwide
5 Weeks
Annual Capacity Reclaimed
The Problem
Five times a year, Staples launches a major seasonal campaign across ~980 retail stores. Each transition required five full-time employees to manually reassign every store's promotional floor plan — consuming roughly one work week per campaign, totaling five weeks of high-effort, low-value labor annually.
My Approach
I identified this as a systems problem masquerading as a staffing problem. I worked with engineering to build a proxy environment where the retail team could plan and map seasonal floor layouts in advance. On go-live, teams simply switched the active feed — no manual reassignment, no lag.
The Outcome
Overnight store transitions, fully automated. All 980 stores receive updated promotional layouts simultaneously at go-live. Five weeks of high-priority manual work across five senior employees was reclaimed each year — allowing the team to focus on strategic initiatives rather than error-prone execution overhead. Downstream, stores also eliminated the added labor cost of correcting manual errors during the previous transition process.
